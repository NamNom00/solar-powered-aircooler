Background of the Study

With the world grappling with rising temperatures due to global warming, the need for sustainable and energy-efficient cooling systems has never been greater. Traditional air conditioning methods consume massive amounts of energy, primarily generated by fossil fuels—further contributing to climate change.

This study introduces a solar-powered air cooler that operates entirely off renewable energy and is constructed using affordable, accessible materials. It aims to offer a solution for communities that face both extreme heat and limited access to reliable electricity.

The Problem: Global Warming

Global warming leads to longer and hotter summers, posing health risks and reducing productivity, especially in tropical and equatorial countries. Most modern cooling systems are expensive, energy-hungry, and often inaccessible to low-income communities. Additionally, they emit greenhouse gases that worsen the problem they are trying to solve.

The Solution: Solar Powered Air Cooler

The proposed solar-powered air cooler is an eco-friendly, cost-efficient device that uses solar panels and evaporative cooling methods to reduce temperatures without relying on the electrical grid. Designed with simplicity in mind, it can be assembled using recycled or easily sourced materials, making it ideal for deployment in underdeveloped or rural areas.

Materials Needed

Solar Panel (12V)

Converts sunlight into electricity to power the fan and pump.

12V Battery

Stores solar energy to run the cooler during low-light periods.

DC Fan

Circulates the cooled air out of the system into the environment.

Water Pump

Pumps water through cooling pads to enable evaporative cooling.

Housing Material

Wood, plastic, or recycled containers to encase the unit.

Cooling Pad

Cellulose or wood wool pads that retain water for cooling effect.
